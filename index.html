About the book

CATKIN HEART

A hopecore, solarpunk fantasy about a mortal woman who stopped killing immortal predators when she realized she was becoming one of them.

The world we have is our choice.

But it is a choice.

An excerpt "

"You owe it to posterity to fucking DIE."

A curved blade in her hand, ripped upward through his immortal guts. She watches him collapse with a sick pleasure. He deserves to die. He deserves to be nothing.

She can see the moment when he realizes, despite all his supposed power, he has no power to stop her. He stares up at her, blood blossoming through his shirt, slow and creeping, like the hatred lacing her soul. The hatred blackening her vision.

Hatred so deep you could forget everything you are.

Taro sat straight up from her nightmare, half expecting to be covered with blood. For a moment, her room was a foreign place. She looked frantically for her blade but her desk held only a stack of notebooks on plant care and creeping tendrils of ivy making the leap from the wall, tracing the entire room like filigree and pirouetting out the window. She laughed at their audacity, remembering where she was now.

She threw herself back into her giant pillow and sighed.

Five years had passed since she'd escaped San Francisco with Kala. Yet even now, with an entire country between her and the immortals, she couldn't escape them in her nightmares.

But that didn't mean she couldn't try for another hour of sleep.

"Helloooooo, our lady of murder, are you awake?" Vega's insistent knocking had woken Taro up every morning this week. Taro kicked herself for agreeing to this. She knew there was no point putting Vega off.

She rubbed the sleep from her eyes and shoved the memories to the back of her mind. She needed to be ready to hold up her end of their deal.

"I'm awake. Come in." Taro sat up and swung her legs off the bed.

"Last day! Told you I'd come every day. So, a deal's a deal." Vega grinned and plopped herself down in Taro's reading hammock.

"Not done yet. The deal wasn't 'wake my ass up every day.' And don't call me that where Kala can hear. Actually, just don't call me that."

"Kala is downstairs already. I would never say it in front of her, I know that. But. It's kinda funny. From murderer to gardener." Vega smirked and rocked herself in the hammock with one foot on the floor.

Taro rolled her eyes. "Don't make me kill you."

— Catkin Heart
